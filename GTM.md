# Go-To-Market

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series-B startup,
15–100 employees, 5–30 engineers. They approve SaaS spend. They pay for
Cursor Business, GitHub Copilot Business/Enterprise, and either ChatGPT
Team or Claude Team — often all three. Monthly AI tool spend: $500–$3,000.

They are not cost-obsessed. They are time-poor. They know they're probably
not perfectly optimized but have never sat down to check because "there's
always something more important." They will act on a specific, credible
recommendation with a dollar amount attached.

**Secondary:** Technical co-founder at a pre-seed or seed startup with
2–8 people. More price-sensitive. Uses 2–3 AI tools personally. More
likely to share the tool in communities.

**Not our user (yet):** Solo developers on $20/mo plans. The audit
produces real findings but the Credex conversion opportunity is near zero.
Don't optimize distribution for this segment.

## What They Google or Scroll Through

Before they'd want this tool, they are:
- Reading "State of AI Tools for Developers" roundups (every 3 months)
- Scrolling through CTO/EM Twitter when someone posts about switching from
  Copilot to Cursor or vice versa
- In a Slack thread where someone says "wait, how much are we spending on
  ChatGPT?" and nobody knows
- Reviewing their AWS/SaaS invoice and wincing at the AI line items
- Reading a thread like "What does your AI tooling stack cost per dev?"

Search queries that would lead them here (before the tool exists):
- "cursor vs copilot cost comparison team"
- "chatgpt team vs claude team pricing"
- "how much should I spend on AI tools per developer"
- "ai tool spend benchmark startup"

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Seed with personal network**

1. Post on Twitter: "Built a free AI spend audit tool this week.
   It checks your Cursor/Copilot/Claude/ChatGPT plan mix and tells you
   exactly what you're overpaying for and why. No login. Takes 60 seconds.
   Here's mine: [share URL showing real savings]." Personal share URL is
   the proof — not a screenshot, the actual live link.

2. Post in Rands Leadership Slack #tools: "Hey — built a free tool for
   auditing AI tool spend this week (was auditing our own and realized
   there was no benchmark). Would love feedback from EMs/CTOs who manage
   this budget." Ask for feedback, not users. Gets more engagement.

3. DM 10 CTOs/EMs from personal network. Not "use my tool" — "I built
   something you might find useful, takes 60 seconds, would value your
   reaction."

**Week 2: Communities**

4. Submit to Hacker News as Show HN. Post between 9–11am PST Tuesday.
   Title: "Show HN: SpendLens – Free AI spend audit for startup teams."
   First comment is your own, explaining the audit engine methodology.

5. Post in IndieHackers: "What I built this week and what I learned
   about AI tool pricing" — narrative post, not product pitch. Include
   the URL at the end.

6. Find 3 active Twitter threads about "Cursor vs Copilot" or "AI tool
   cost" in the past 30 days. Reply with: "Built something relevant to
   this — [URL]. No pitch, just found this thread while building it."

**Week 3–4: Amplify what's working**

7. Whichever of the above drove the most signups — double down there.
8. Ask the first 10 email captures "would you share this with your eng
   team?" — word of mouth within a company is the strongest signal.

**The unfair distribution channel:**

Credex already has relationships with customers who buy AI credits. Every
Credex customer is a SpendLens user — they got credits because they were
overspending. Email the existing Credex customer base: "We built a free
audit tool. Run it on your current stack and see what you're still leaving
on the table." This is the channel only Credex has.
