# Economics

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits. A typical deal:

- Customer is spending $1,000/mo on Cursor Business (25 seats × $40)
- Credex offers the same access at ~$750/mo (25% discount is conservative
  estimate based on "sourced from companies that overforecast")
- Customer saves $250/mo, $3,000/year
- Credex margin on the deal: roughly 10–15% of face value = $75–$112/mo
  = $900–$1,350/year per customer

**Conservative LTV estimate:**

Average customer contract: 12 months (annual commitment for the discount
to make sense). Average margin per customer: $1,000/year.

Churn: ~25% annually (customers switch tools, companies fold, budgets cut).
LTV = $1,000 / 0.25 = **$4,000 per converted customer**

This is conservative. A 50-seat enterprise deal at $40/seat = $2,000/mo
face value. At 10% margin = $200/mo = $2,400/year. LTV at 25% churn =
$9,600 from one customer.

## CAC by Channel

| Channel | Estimated CAC | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Organic Twitter (personal) | $0 | No paid spend; time cost only |
| Hacker News Show HN | $0 | No paid spend |
| Rands/Eng Leadership Slack | $0 | No paid spend |
| Existing Credex customer base | $0 | Already have the relationship |
| Referral from SpendLens share URL | $0 | Viral loop built into product |
| LinkedIn outbound (manual DMs) | ~$50/lead | ~2 hrs/week of founder time @ $25/hr equivalent |
| Paid Twitter/X ads (if we ever run them) | ~$200–400/lead | Estimated; not in current plan |

**Effective blended CAC at launch:** ~$20–40 per qualified lead
(accounting for founder time on distribution). Near-zero cash CAC.

Qualified lead → Credex consultation booked: estimated 20% (1 in 5 people
who left email and saw ≥$200/mo savings will take a free 20-minute call).

Consultation booked → credit purchase: estimated 25% (1 in 4 calls
converts; qualified lead already self-selected with real spend data).

**Effective CAC per paying Credex customer:**
$30 blended lead CAC / (20% × 25%) = $30 / 0.05 = **$600 per customer**

LTV:CAC = $4,000 : $600 = **6.7x** — well above the 3x minimum for a
healthy SaaS acquisition motion.

## Conversion Funnel

```
Visitors to SpendLens
        ↓ 40% complete the audit form
Audit completions
        ↓ 35% show ≥ $200/mo savings  (high-savings case)
High-savings audits
        ↓ 40% leave email
Qualified leads (email captured, ≥ $200 savings)
        ↓ 20% book Credex consultation
Consultations booked
        ↓ 25% purchase credits
Paying Credex customers
```

**Example: 1,000 monthly visitors**

| Stage | Number | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Visitors | 1,000 | — |
| Audit completions | 400 | 40% |
| High-savings audits | 140 | 35% of completions |
| Qualified leads | 56 | 40% of high-savings |
| Consultations | 11 | 20% of leads |
| Customers | 3 | 25% of consultations |

3 new customers/month × $4,000 LTV = **$12,000 LTV added per month**
from 1,000 monthly visitors.

Break-even on the tool build cost (this project): at $1,000/year per
customer margin, we need 4–5 customers to cover a month of eng time.
That's achievable in month 1 if distribution works.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

$1M ARR = 1,000 Credex customers at $1,000/year margin each
(or 500 customers at $2,000/year — plausible with larger accounts)

**What has to be true:**

1. **Traffic:** SpendLens needs to drive ~5,000 audit completions/month
   by month 12. That requires:
   - 12,500 monthly visitors (40% completion rate)
   - Sustained distribution: HN, Twitter, community Slacks, SEO
     on "AI tool pricing" and "Cursor vs Copilot cost" queries

2. **Conversion rate holds:** The 40% email capture and 20%
   consultation booking rates are estimates. If they're half of that
   (20% and 10%), we need double the traffic. If they're better,
   the math improves.

3. **Credex closes deals:** The tool generates leads; Credex closes them.
   The consultation → purchase rate (25%) needs to hold at volume.
   This depends on Credex's sales motion, not SpendLens.

4. **Deal size grows:** The model above uses small accounts ($1,000/yr
   margin). One 50-seat enterprise deal = $2,400/yr margin. Targeting
   engineering leaders at 30–100 person companies (via Rands Slack etc.)
   tilts the mix toward larger accounts.

**Month-by-month model (rough):**

| 1 | 500 | 1 | $1,000 |
| 3 | 1,500 | 4 | $15,000 |

Inputs are rough but the direction is clear: the tool is viable if
